A pasta dish with marinara sauce

Pasta with marinara is a classic Italian dish that consists of pasta tossed in a tomato-based sauce.

The sauce is made with crushed tomatoes, garlic, herbs, and seasonings, and is simmered until thick and flavorful.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of pasta (spaghetti, linguine, or your preferred type)
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can of crushed tomatoes (28 ounces)
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon of red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Parmesan cheese, grated (optional)

Steps

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat.
  3. Add the chopped onion and cook until soft and translucent, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  5. Add the crushed tomatoes, salt, black pepper, dried basil, dried oregano, and red pepper flakes (if using). Stir to combine.
  6. Bring the sauce to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  8. Drain the cooked pasta and return it to the pot.
  9. Pour the marinara sauce over the pasta and toss to coat.
  10. Serve with grated Parmesan cheese, if desired.
