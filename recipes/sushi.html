Sushi

Basic maki sushi rolls

Learn how to make a seafood dish

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of sushi rice
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1/4 cup of rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 4 sheets of nori seaweed
  • Assorted fillings (such as avocado, cucumber, carrot, crab meat, cooked shrimp, smoked salmon, etc.)
  • Soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger (optional)

Steps

  1. Rinse the sushi rice in a fine mesh strainer until the water runs clear.
  2. In a medium saucepan, combine the rice and water and bring to a boil over high heat.
  3. Reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan, and simmer for 18-20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the water has been absorbed.
  4. In a small saucepan, combine the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Heat over low heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar and salt have dissolved.
  5. Transfer the cooked rice to a large mixing bowl and add the vinegar mixture. Use a wooden spoon or spatula to gently fold the rice until it is evenly coated with the vinegar mixture. Allow the rice to cool to room temperature.
  6. Lay a sheet of nori seaweed on a sushi mat or a clean kitchen towel with the shiny side facing down.
  7. Wet your hands with water and scoop about 1/2 cup of sushi rice onto the nori sheet. Use your fingers to spread the rice evenly over the nori, leaving a 1-inch border at the top.
  8. Arrange your desired fillings in a line across the center of the rice.
  9. Use the sushi mat or kitchen towel to tightly roll the sushi away from you, using the border of rice at the top to seal the roll.
  10. Repeat with the remaining nori sheets and fillings.
  11. Use a sharp knife to slice the sushi rolls into bite-sized pieces.
  12. Serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger, if desired.
